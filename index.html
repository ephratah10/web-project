Chess Home

Chess, a game of remarkable depth and history, boasts intriguing facts that add layers to its allure. With over 288 billion possible ways to play the first four moves per side, the complexity of the game is staggering. The longest recorded game, lasting 269 moves and over 20 hours, ended in a draw. Notably, the queen, once limited to moving one square at a time, gained its formidable powers in the 15th century. The knight stands as the sole piece capable of jumping over others on the board..

Chess Rules

Chess is a two-player strategy board game. The objective is to checkmate the opponent's king, putting it under an inescapable threat of capture.

Piece Move
King One square in any direction
Queen Any number of squares in any direction
Rook Any number of squares horizontally or vertically
Bishop Any number of of squares diagonally
Knight In an "L" shape: two squares in one direction and then one square perpendicular
Pawn Forward one square but captures diagonally. On its first move, a pawn has the option to move forward two squares
